Timeline

Spring 20

Currently. We are currently focusing on establishing our database with pre-existing labeled and unlabeled data, as well as automating the acquisition of previously-untapped file sources.

Fall 19

Contacts. Much of our Fall was spent getting in touch with experts across fields. The majority of our group members have academic backgrounds in computational science/mathematics and research/development, while ASL translation requires knowledge from a greater number of ranging fields. By the end of the Fall, with contacts established and a lot of information gained, we could confidently formalize our next steps in database development and judging ML model approaches.

09/15/19

University Backing. Dispersal home to our individual institutions. Organization for remote collaboration with lab personnel and early timelines established.

08/01/19

Shark Tank Round. Final 6 teams pitch. Judges include Lab Director, Division leaders, and Air Force personnel. ENVOY recieves first-place victory, securing project funding for implementation.

07/16/19

Poster Presentation Round. In-person voting from technical staff. We secure the first-place, 'Best Poster' award, and a spot in the final Shark Tank round.

06/18/19

Project Pitch Stage. Virtual polling takes place. Voted through to the next round of the I3C Challenge by Lab personnel.

06/01/19

Origin. Our group was formed in the early stages of the Intern Innovation Idea Challenge (I3C), held for interns taking part in MIT Lincoln Laboratory's Summer Research Program.
