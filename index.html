Research

Welcome. We're a group of technical researchers at MIT Lincoln Laboratory. Our project is ENVOY: Enabling Natural-language Opportunity & Versatility. With support from a growing list of collaborators, our group explores questions surrounding improving the machine translation of American Sign Language (ASL).

Achieving full ASL translation requires interdisciplinary cooperation. The fields of machine learning (ML), computer vision, human-computer interaction, and linguistics (to name a few) must integrate fluidly. Our group intends to bridge implicit gaps of domain-specific understanding, and to build comprehensive translatory system designs for researchers to aim towards.

Database Contribution

One of the critical issues holding back research in the machine translation of signed languages is the lack of quality signing data, which is necessary to generate and improve the performance of ML models.

To address this, our group is currently developing a large database that merges existing research datasets with ASL training videos and other publically-available data of ASL in practice (i.e. Government, open-source sites). To generate powerful working models, we need as much data as possible.

If you are an organization, research group, or institution interested in collaborating with us to meet our database goals, please let us know. Data provided is for non-profit, solely research, purposes.
